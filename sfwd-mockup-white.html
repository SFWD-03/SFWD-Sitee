Choosing the Right Company

What to Ask Before You
Sign Any Contract.

The window replacement industry in South Florida has no shortage of fly-by-night contractors. Here's how to protect yourself.

Ask for their license number
Florida requires a state contractor's license to pull permits for window replacement. Verify at myfloridalicense.com before signing anything. Ours: CGC1519583.
Confirm they pull permits
Unpermitted window work can void your insurance, create issues at resale, and expose you to code violations. Always confirm permits are included — we handle them on every job.
Ask if they use subcontractors
Many large companies outsource installations. Our crew is fully employed and trained in-house — you get the same quality control on every job, every time.
Check their product approvals
Every window installed in Florida must carry a product approval number issued by the state. Ask to see the NOA (Notice of Acceptance) for any window they propose.

Hurricane Protection

Why South Florida Homes
Need Impact Windows.

South Florida sits in one of the most active hurricane corridors in the world. Standard windows — even "storm-resistant" models — can fail under the pressure spikes and debris impacts that Category 2+ storms produce. When a window fails, it's not just glass. It's a sudden pressure change that can structurally compromise your entire roof system.

Hurricane impact windows are built with laminated glass bonded to a polymer interlayer that holds together even when shattered — the same technology used in automotive windshields. When you're looking for hurricane impact windows near you in South Florida, choosing a certified, experienced installer is as critical as choosing the right product.

The Technology

How Hurricane Impact Windows
Actually Protect Your Home

Laminated Impact Glass

Two or more panes of tempered glass are bonded with a PVB or SGP interlayer. On impact, the glass cracks but the interlayer holds — keeping the window intact and your home sealed against wind and water.

Reinforced Aluminum Frames

Heavy-duty extruded aluminum frames are engineered to resist wind loads up to 200+ mph. Frames are thermally broken for added energy efficiency and sealed with marine-grade weatherstripping.

How much do hurricane impact windows cost in South Florida?

Do I need a permit to install hurricane impact windows in Florida?

Yes. All hurricane impact window installations in Florida require a building permit from your county. We handle the entire permitting process, including application, inspections, and final sign-off — at no additional charge.

Will hurricane impact windows lower my homeowners insurance?

Almost certainly. Florida law requires insurers to offer discounts for certified opening protection. Savings vary by insurer and the number of windows replaced, but reductions of 20–45% on the windstorm portion of your premium are common. Ask us about My Safe Florida Home grant assistance.

How long does it take to install impact windows?

Most single-room installations take one day. Full-home projects typically take 2–5 days depending on the number of openings and complexity. Lead time from order to installation is typically 6–10 weeks after permit approval.
